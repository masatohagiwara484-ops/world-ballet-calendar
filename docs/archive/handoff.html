1 · What this product is

A single, beautiful place to discover ballet & opera worldwide with deep, interconnected metadata. Audience = affluent, high-travel-intent culture lovers who can't currently search across dozens of siloed opera-house sites. The structural opportunity: each house only lists its own shows; nobody lets you search the world by work, artist, or city.

"Every stage in the world. One search."
Where can I see Swan Lake this year? — one page, every company.
Show me everything Nureyev choreographed, playing in Paris in October, under €80.

This is why the literal name "World Ballet Calendar" undersells it — it's a knowledge graph, not a calendar.

4 · Monetization plan

Ads are out. The pillars, in priority order:

  • A · Ticket on-sale alerts — the subscription core. Popular runs sell out in hours; "notify me when tickets go on sale" is the one thing this audience pays for.
  • B · "Performance Trip" bundled affiliate — hotels near the venue (Booking/Expedia deep-linked by lat/lng), flights, and Tiqets / GetYourGuide tours (best fit). Replaces the fake partners page.
  • C · Newsletter + sponsorship — weekly "This Week on Stage"; high sponsor CPMs + retention funnel. Email capture = #1 CTA site-wide.
  • D · B2B featured listings — houses pay to feature seasons / tours.
  • E · Data licensing / API (long-term) — the normalized world performance graph is itself the asset.

Subscription — Free vs Grand Tier (~$6/mo · $48/yr)

5 · Architecture — the entity graph

The old data was flat (free-text composer/choreographer strings) — unsearchable by artist. We introduced a normalized graph:

people        choreographers, composers, dancers, conductors… (de-duped by slug)
works         the abstract piece — "Swan Lake" — merged across ALL companies
venues        one per company location (first-class for multi-venue later)
productions   a staging: work × company × choreographer
performances  the scheduled run — the searchable unit
performance_credits   performance ↔ person + role  ← powers "search any artist"

Key choice: the graph is built deterministically from the existing data at runtime (src/lib/graph.ts), so search works today; the SAME shape is mirrored by the Supabase schema (003_entity_graph.sql) for when real scraped data lands. The shared normalizer means a scraped "Kenneth MacMillan" resolves to the same id as a seeded one.

Filters: free-text, discipline, country, city, company, person (any role), choreographer, composer, work, date range, price band (cross-currency via EUR), sort, pagination — plus live facet counts & autocomplete.

Verified working on the real seed: "Swan Lake" → 14 companies worldwide; "Kenneth MacMillan" → his productions in London & New York; price + facet filters correct.
